Overview

The C2A assessment battery measures 12 durable skills from the LE3 Graduate Pillars Framework using 14 validated psychometric instruments totaling 144 items. The battery is organized into four coaching lenses — a framework designed around what coaches need to know, in what order, to make effective decisions about student support.

Lens 1: Executive Function

Purpose: Profile the cognitive and regulatory capacities that shape how a student learns, adapts, and self-regulates. Strong EF is an asset to build on; EF difficulties are constraints that require accommodation in coaching approach.

Differential Diagnosis

When a student scores low on a durable skill, the coach's first question should not be "how do I teach this skill?" but "why is this skill where it is?" The answer determines the intervention.

The four-lens structure makes this possible. A single-instrument skill measurement tells you that a student is struggling — the multi-instrument battery tells you why. The Executive Function lens is checked first because EF capacity changes the interpretation of everything downstream.

Worked Example: Creative Problem Solving

Creative Problem Solving is measured primarily by the SFCQ (Knowledge + Skills subscales) with supporting information from CFS (cognitive flexibility) and CAAS-SF Curiosity. When a student scores low, the battery enables the coach to distinguish three distinct profiles:

Profile A: Cognitive Constraint

Pattern: Low WM + Low Cognitive Flexibility + Normal/High Curiosity

What's happening: The student is curious, but working memory limitations prevent them from holding multiple solutions simultaneously. They appear "stuck" due to capacity, not lack of ideas.

Coaching approach: Scaffold externally. Use visual organizers and decision matrices. Do NOT insist they "think harder."

Profile B: Dispositional Barrier

Pattern: Normal WM + Low Cognitive Flexibility + Low Curiosity

What's happening: Adequate capacity, but low flexibility/curiosity creates a dispositional barrier. They resist exploring novel approaches.

Coaching approach: Build tolerance for ambiguity. Check Lens 3 for motivation.

Profile C: Exploratory Deficit

Pattern: Normal WM + Normal Flexibility + Low Curiosity + Low SFCQ-Knowledge

What's happening: Adequate infrastructure, but lacks the drive and cultural knowledge base.

Coaching approach: Ignite curiosity through exposure to diverse perspectives and real-world problems.

Administration Recommendations

Three-Sitting Semester Design

The battery is administered across three sittings per semester. Sittings are broken into "sprints" to minimize cognitive fatigue.

SittingTimingContentItemsEst. Time
T1Start of semesterEF + All Skills + SDT + SFCQ + IRI~14535–45 min (3 sprints)
T2Mid-semesterCore Skills only~5915–20 min (2 sprints)
T3End of semesterEF + Core Skills + SDT + IRI + Contextual~13425–35 min (3 sprints)

Why T1a/T3a first? It gives the coach the EF capacity profile and motivational baseline immediately. If a student shows high amotivation or significant WM difficulties in the first sprint, the coach already knows the profile is shaped by those factors before skill scores come in.

Scoring Engine Requirements

  • Score carry-forward: T2 reports display the most recent EF and SDT scores from T1 alongside current skill scores.
  • Timestamps per lens: Each score carries its collection date (e.g., "EF scores from T1 — 6 weeks ago").
  • Three-point trajectory: The engine supports slope/trend calculation across T1/T2/T3.
  • Partial battery handling: The system treats missing instruments at T2 as by-design, not as missing data.
